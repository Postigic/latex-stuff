\begin{claim}
    The number of odd-degree vertices in a simple graph is even.
\end{claim}

\begin{proof}
    Let $G$ be a simple graph. We first sum all the vertex degrees. By the Handshaking Lemma:
    \[
    \sum_{v \in V} \deg(v) = 2|E|
    \]
    Hence the sum is even.

    \medskip

    Let $V_e$ be the set of vertices in $G$ of even degree, and let $V_o$ be the set of vertices 
    in $G$ of odd degree. Then:
    \[
    \sum_{v \in V} \deg(v) = \sum_{v \in V_e} \deg(v) + \sum_{v \in V_o} \deg(v)
    \]
    We know that $\sum_{v \in V} \deg(v)$ is even, and $\sum_{v \in V_e} \deg(v)$ is also even 
    as the sum of even numbers is even. Hence $\sum_{v \in V_o} \deg(v)$ must be even as well.
    Each term in $\sum_{v \in V_o} \deg(v)$ is odd, so the sum has the same parity as $|V_o|$. 
    Since the sum is even, $|V_o|$ is even.

    \medskip

    Hence the number of odd-degree vertices in $G$ is even.
\end{proof}
